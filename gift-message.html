A message from my side

Six months ago you walked into Jio with a bag on your shoulder and a quiet kind of nervous energy that only I could see. And today, I'm sitting here trying to find words that are big enough for everything you've become — and I'm not sure any exist. The canteen food that somehow tasted better every time. The lake that turned ordinary lunch breaks into something that felt like a scene from a film you'd want to rewatch. The view from up there — the kind that makes you stop mid-sentence just to breathe it in. The laughs that came out of nowhere and the shenanigans that probably shouldn't be written down anywhere. Anish and Sahil — people you didn't know six months ago and now can't imagine the chapter without. That's the thing about the right places — they give you the right people. But more than all of that — I watched you find a version of yourself you hadn't met before. Quieter in the best way. Sharper. More sure. The kind of sure that doesn't need to announce itself. I am so incredibly proud of you. Not just for finishing — but for how you showed up every single day, even the hard ones. And I'm not going anywhere. Whatever comes next — the big dreams, the uncertain mornings, the days you doubt yourself — I'll be right there, same as always. Keep believing in yourself the way I believe in you. Work harder than yesterday, be kinder to yourself than last week, and never stop chasing the version of you that's still becoming. I love you. More than this frame can hold. 🌿
